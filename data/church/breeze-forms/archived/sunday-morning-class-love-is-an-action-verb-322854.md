# Sunday Morning Class: "Love is an action verb"

*archived* · 8 entries · last entry 2020-10-30 · created 2020-10-14

## Fields

1. _paragraph_ — We are excited that you'll be joining our Sunday morning class! Beginning October 18, we’ll meet via Zoom each Sunday from 9am - 10:10am for five weeks. Why does Paul say that love is more important than faith or hope? What do the Jesus’ actions and words teach us about loving ALL people, even those who abuse their power and position or refuse to respect others? Why do some modern theologians argue that love is the greatest dynamic force for goodness and hope, and the antidote to the division and chaos in our worlds? In the end, what does this complex “love” have to do with me – with my spiritual life, my relationships, my responsibilities, my survival? For five weeks beginning Sunday, October 18, our Sunday morning adult spiritual enrichment class will wrestle with these questions through a study of love, or agape . Resources for our discussions will include videos of sermons by Bishop Michael Curry, Residing Bishop of the Episcopal Church. (Yes, that’s the amazing preacher from the royal; wedding of Harry and Meghan.) We’ll also read excerpts from his latest book, Love is the Way. (Getting the book is highly recommended but not required.) And we’ll revisit well-known stories of Jesus’ teachings from the Gospels and refer often to Paul’s descriptions of love in I Corinthians 13. Connection information for our class will be sent to you once you register below.
