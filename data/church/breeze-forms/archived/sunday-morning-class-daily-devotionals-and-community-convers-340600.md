# Sunday Morning Class: DAILY DEVOTIONALS AND COMMUNITY CONVERSATION

*archived* · 12 entries · last entry 2020-12-05 · created 2020-11-18

## Fields

1. _paragraph_ — We are excited that you'll be joining our Sunday morning Advent class! We'll be meeting Sundays, November 29 - December 20, at 9AM via Zoom . Advent is a time of waiting, but not inactivity. This Advent season we’ll focus on the spiritual discipline of daily devotionals, followed by Sunday morning discussions of what we have learned and how we’re being strengthened for the work ahead. We’ll be using a devotional booklet written by local pastor Jenny Smith (Edmonds UMC) to help us prepare to celebrate Christ’s birth. Readings for each day include the scripture, a short devotion, and a brief prayer. On Sunday mornings, our group leader will guide us in reflecting together on how we can apply the week’s messages to our lives in an Advent season unlike any we have experienced before. Much like the Lenten devotionals we’ve used in the past, this is an opportunity to feel spiritually connected through our common practice, despite this year’s physical separations. Booklets will be mailed out before thanksgiving. Connection information for our class will be sent to you once you register below.

5. _paragraph_ — To ensure authentic conversation and respectful interactions, we ask all participants in meetings and discussions sponsored by First United Methodist Church of Seattle to abide by the following guidelines. 1. Approach each conversation with a contemplative, curious spirit and a willingness to have your heart and mind opened to new ways of thinking. 2. Before you speak, examine your own assumptions, thoughts, and emotions. 3. We are all equal partners in the conversation. Be considerate and respectful of others’ beliefs and ideas, and refrain from attempting to rescue, fix, psychoanalyze, or evaluate another person. 4. Be sensitive to differences in communication styles, cultural norms, and comfort levels. 5. Constructive disagreement and differences of opinion are welcome. Personal attacks and deliberately provocative comments are not allowed. 6. There is absolutely no tolerance of racism, sexism, homophobia, profanity, or any form of hate speech. All offensive or threatening comments will be removed, and the participant may be banned from further virtual meetings.
