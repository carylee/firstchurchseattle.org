We come before you this day in trembling and trepidation. For the hour grows dark and our spirits are burdened with failing hopes and growing anxieties.

Grant us wisdom enough to realize the world changing power that resides within our freedom to choose how we will respond.

Author of life, grant us this day what we need for the living of this day.

Help us grow in trust of you, realizing that all we have and all we need and all we are, we receive as gift from you each day of our lives.

Help us to know beyond our ideas, to feel more deeply than our emotions, and to be fundamentally changed by that which is unchanging. Help us to see beyond the turmoil, the rights and wrongs, the good guys and the villains, to the radiant heart of things.

Deliver us from the stories of inadequacy and fear that we tell ourselves and then believe, the endless iterations of self-centered fear, which we torment ourselves with like children in the dark.

Rather, let us walk in your light, the light of the truth that you are always with us, in all times, in all places, in all experiences, and that nothing, absolutely nothing, can separate us from your gentle, generous, generative love.

And finally, we lift up all of your children, human and more than human, who suffer at the hands of the powerful and the privileged. May we be mindful of the oppressed and the dispossessed, the unhomed and the deranged, the tormented and neglected, the outcast, the forgotten, and the shamed.

May love prevail, and may justice roll down like mighty waters.

And let us close with a prayer from the pen and the heart of the Reverend Howard Thurman.

Oh love of God, without which life has no meaning and no harbor, leave us not alone with our little lives, our broken dreams, our insistent problems, but invade our spirit with your vitality, that we may be renewed in all the ways of our lives, that we may turn from this place, this day, with all that is within us, washed and purified and refreshed.

We seek this with simplicity of heart, and with quiet faith and confidence that you would not deny your love to your children.
