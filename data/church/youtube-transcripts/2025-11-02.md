**Jerry Roberson:**
Good morning, First Church, and thank you for this opportunity to speak today on a topic that's close to my heart as we continue through this stewardship season. For me, sacred experiences is at the heart of everything that we do as Christians. As we pray, as we fellowship, as we serve, as we learn together, we are worshipping God. And all of those experiences are sacred. Even if we didn't have a building, or a denomination, or an organized congregation with staff, we still have sacred experiences together. However, with all of those things I've just listed, our experiences can be lived so much more to the fullest. We can have spaces that are warm in the winter or cool in the summer, where we can invite our friends to come experience God with us. We can have curriculum to aid our Sunday school classes to thoughtfully and methodically work our way through God's word. To the best, to best understand how we are called to live and to serve as followers of Christ. And we can support pastors and staff who bring specialized skills, experience, and knowledge to the table, shepherding and addressing needs, and encouraging spiritual gifts that we may not even know that we have. Here at First Church, sacred experiences encompass a range of ministry areas that include corporate worship, like we do here this morning, spiritual formation, congregational development, and youth and children. Each of those areas are tasked with their own specific charge towards making disciples of Jesus Christ for the transformation of the world.

I've had the great pleasure of being part of the worship team here at First Church for the past few years. We meet, we plan together alongside our pastors and Elizabeth and the members on our team to envision how our worship opportunities together can be the most inviting and the most fulfilling.
