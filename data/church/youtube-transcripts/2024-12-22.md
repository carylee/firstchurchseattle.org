### Welcome to Lessons and Carols - “A Song of Peace"
**Rev. Jackie Celin:**
We move now into our time of lessons and Carols. Our service of Lessons and Carols today comes out of a very long standing Christian tradition. Dating back to the early 1900s, as a response to the devastation of the Great War, World War I. The first service of nine Lessons and Carols was heard in 1918 at King's College Chapel in Cambridge. Its roots go even further back to Christmas Eve in in 1880, when Bishop Benson Benson created a little service of nine Carols and tiny lessons, called the Festal Service for Christmas Eve. Eric Milner-White, the Dean of King's College, created the tradition out of these roots that we know and love today. Milner-White had been the chaplain at King's College in 1912. He was then called to the Western Front and to Italy during the Great War, and returned in 1918, when he was then made Dean of King's College. The lessons and throughout the service tell of God's redemption story through use of the familiar Christmas story. Each lesson is followed by a song either sung by the choir, the congregation, other musicians, or a combination of all. The candle lit sanctuary reminds us that the peace, love, hope, and joy of Christ and of the Christmas season shines brighter amongst the pain, suffering, and brokenness of our world. As the preface to the 2018 Festivals Order of Service states, "Wherever the service is heard, and however it is adapted, whether the music is provided by choir or congregation, the main theme is the development of the loving purposes of God, seen through the windows and the words of the Bible." Our sanctuary choir will lead us today through our service of Lessons and Carols with Craig and Sean providing the readings. Many of the Carols will be sung as anthems by the choir, and you are invited to join in where the text is bolded on the slides and for the congregational hymns. And now I will say Milner-White's bidding prayer. This is a prayer that begins each service of Lessons and Carols, which he wrote to both recognize the grief that many experienced because of all the lives lost in the war, while also offering hope. As you listen to the words that I will speak, think about the ways we are connected to all others participating in this Christian service of Lessons and Carols throughout the world this Advent season. That begins that begin their services with this same bidding prayer. Think about the peace, hope, and love that comes with Christ to our broken world as we seek to find our place in God's redemption story. You are now invited to take a form of prayer that's most comfortable to you.

### Reading: "It Came Upon a Midnight Clear" — Edmund H. Sears
**Peter Jabin:**
It came upon a midnight clear, that glorious song of old, From angels bending near the earth to touch their harps of gold. "Peace on the earth, good will to men, from Heaven's all gracious King." The world in solemn stillness lay to hear the angels sing. Still through the cloven skies they come, with peaceful wings unfurled, And ever o'er our Babel-world proclaim the blessed morn. O hark the glad good news: "Behold, the world is hushed and still, And man, at midnight, can find rest in peace on earth, good will." For lo, the days are hastening on, by prophet seen of old, When with the ever circling year shall come the time foretold. When peace shall over all the earth, its ancient splendors fling, And the whole world send back the song, which now the angels sing.
