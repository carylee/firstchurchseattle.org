### Opening & Invitation to Lenten Discipline
**Rev. Wongee Joh:**
Welcome friends, to our Ash Wednesday service. All are welcome here, and all always means all. I'm Pastor Wongee Jo, pronouns she/her, serving here at First Church. Whether you are participating in person or friends online now or anytime later this evening or even this week, may you receive this invitation for a quiet for to quiet our souls and to wander into this holy season with all that we are.

For the imposition of ashes at home, please have prepared some symbolic material representative of our mortality, like a dab of garden soil. I invite you to be creative in embracing the spiritual meaning behind our symbolic imposition of ashes, even writing down the words for our friends online, "You are dust and to dust you shall return."

Let us begin with wonder and expectation of what might come. Lent begins with the disorientation of finding our places in the world at this moment. The ground beneath us has shifted, and we are invited to consider whether and where we might go next. Let's begin this Lent traveling with the colors of our heart. Let us map the possibilities that God holds before us now. Let us bring the words of Antonio Machado in his writing, Campos de Castilla.

"Wanderer, your footsteps are the road, and nothing more. Wanderers, there is no road. The road is made by walking. By walking, and by walking one makes the road, and upon glancing behind, one sees the path that never be trod again."

We take our first steps into the possibility of resurrection and hope by reflecting on God's call in our lives and looking for what might rise from the ashes of death and destruction. We have already wandered so long and so far, and cannot imagine what might happen next. So we come with trust, us surrendering into our God who leads us ever forward. Let us begin with welcoming one another with the peace of Christ.
