## Las Posadas (Shelter for the Holy Family)
[00:00]
### Welcome and Introduction
**Pastor Jackie:**
As we move into our Las Posadas program, we invite uh children and youth at this time to meet April uh back by the big doors.

Las Posadas is a time of celebration in Mexico and the southwestern United States. During the celebration, pilgrims journey from house to house led by angels, symbolizing the journey of Mary and Joseph who went from house to house seeking shelter. Finally, the last house, the inn, welcomes all of the pilgrims in for prayers, feasting, and festivities. Las Posadas is a Christian, biblical, and evangelistic service out of the Hispanic culture and typically takes place for eight nights from December 16th through December 23rd with a special service on Christmas Eve.

Each year, groups also gather at the wall between San Diego and Tijuana where the border meets the ocean to do a joint service on both sides of the wall, called Las Posadas in Fronteras, the inn without borders, to reflect on the hope that one day we will tear down the divisions we have created, remembering that we are all God's beloved.

This service is a reimagining of the journey of the Holy Family, of their time spent in Bethlehem seeking shelter and food. In our worship service today, we are asking for full participation from all of you from the congregation, both here in the sanctuary and online. The intention is to imagine we are there and a part of this story, and also to consider how this story relates to our contemporary time and place. This is an Advent service. We are preparing and anticipating the birth of Jesus as we reenact and remember the moments right before Jesus's birth.

As our Holy Family, our children and youth make their way to each door, there will be a conversation that takes place between Mary and Joseph, accompanied by shepherds and angels, and the people inside the home. You, the congregation, will fill the role of the people who answer the door, reminding us of our responsibility to welcome all that seek asylum and refuge, no matter how far from the borders we live. We invite you to participate with your whole hearts as we enter into this moment of Advent.
