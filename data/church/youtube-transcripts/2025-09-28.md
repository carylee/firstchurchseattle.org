We pray that your love would be born in us and overflow from us.

Eternal life-giver, gracious creator, as we gather before you in prayer this glorious autumn morning. Let's pause to consider what we understand when we use this word, “we.” Who is this “we” that prays? To be honest, at our most generous, we might mean all of humankind. But we ask this morning that you expand our hearts and our imaginations so that our “we” might include the whales and the wildebeests, the bees and the bear, the salmon and the sparrows. Expand our hearts and our imaginations so that our “we” might include the grasses and the glaciers, the cedars and the storks, the mountains and the mycelium. Expand our hearts and our imaginations so that our “we” might include sunshine and moonlight, clouds and wind, fire and flame, mountains, thunder, rivers, hills. Teach us to pray, “we the peoples, we the living, we the world.”

Creator of creatures, blessed be your name. We the congregation of animals, praise you for the furred and the feathered, the scaled and the slippery, all manner of beast on earth and in the sky and under the water. We thank you for giving each of us a home where we could thrive. We mourn together for our sisters and brothers, the Spix's Macaw, the Northern White Rhino, so many others, who because of human greed and indifference, will never look upon this earth again. We believe the psalmist who declares, “You save humans and animals alike, great Lamb of God.” Gather us under your wings like a mother hen. Praise be to you who sustains and restores us all.

Mothering God, you formed us from the soil and breathed life into our bones. Thank you for our bodies and our earthly companions of flora and fauna. We are grateful that you chose to teach us how to be human by becoming a human among us. We confess that we have often abandoned our first calling to care for our Earth home. Forgive us for the ways we have wounded creation and our human kin who most feel the effects of a changing climate. Open our hearts to wonder and transformation. Teach us to rebel against the silencing of your creatures, the felling of your forests, and the suffering of your children. Help us to become ambassadors of reconciliation on our way to a new creation. And hear these words from St. Basil the Great of the 4th century, arguably Christianity's first environmentalist. O God, enlarge within us, the sense of fellowship with all living things, brother beasts, sister trees, father rock and mother seas, to whom thou gave us the earth as their home in common with us. May we realize that all creatures live not for us alone, but for themselves and for thee, and that they love the sweetness of life. Amen.
