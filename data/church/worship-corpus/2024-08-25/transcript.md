**D.S. Derek Nakano:**
I bring you greetings from Bishop Cedrick Bridgefort, and it's so good to be with you in worship this morning, and just so grateful this morning for the gift that we have shared together in worship. So grateful and thankful as we have heard the word, as we have shared in song, as we've heard and felt the Spirit alive in this place. We know of the gift of prayer, and the gratitude that our hearts are filled with as we think about that prayer that has sustained us and blessed us throughout our lives. For the ways that that prayer has come to us in those moments when we have felt shipwrecked, in those times when we've experienced joy, we've lifted up our prayers and thanksgiving to God. And we know as well that there have been many prayers of others for us that have lifted us up and sustained us in our lives. We know that that is true about our individual lives. We know that's true about our community. We know as well that it is true about this church. As we think about the generations who have held up this ministry by the power of their prayers, we know that your prayers as well sustain and bless this ministry. And as we go from this place, the question I would put before you is, as we have been blessed by that prayer, as it is filled with us with deep gratitude, as it has supported this community and our city as well. The question that I leave you with is, whose answer, who who will you be as there are others who are lifting up their prayers? Will you be the answer to another's prayer? As you think about those who are experiencing brokenness, those who are hurting in their lives, those who suffer each day, uh as they face oppression and hardship and suffering and struggle. Will you be an answer to their prayer? My prayer this day is that as we have shared on the around the communion table, as we've experienced the love of Christ, that we might go into the world to share that love of Christ as a blessing and know that in that love of Christ, you will be the answer to another's prayer. And know that that is the power of the gift of the Spirit amongst us. And so with that blessing, let us go forth from this place by the power of that Spirit, knowing that God is sending us forth into the world, uh to bring about the kind of transformation that will bring about God's reign here on earth. Amen.
