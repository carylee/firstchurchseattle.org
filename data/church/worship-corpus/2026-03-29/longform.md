* **Land Acknowledgement**  
  * We acknowledge that we are on the unceded traditional homelands of the Duwamish, the first people of Seattle. We honor and respect members of the Duwamish Tribe, past and present, as well as the land and waters we share. We strive to live consciously and respectfully with one another, mindful of the sacredness of all God’s creation.  
* Setting the stage: Today we move from Palm Sunday at the beginning of the service to Passion Sunday (preparing for Holy Week) by the end. There’s a shift in the story in this next hour, and it means we’ll end the service differently: there won’t be any announcements, no postlude, no song of blessing. At the end of the service, you’ll be invited to sit and pray if you’d like, and leave the sanctuary silently when you’re ready. Just save your conversation for the narthex/lobby in case others are praying and reflecting on Holy Week. So here are some announcements to keep in mind, and please refer to the announcements in your bulletin and the e-news that comes out on Friday for more…

**Confession and Pardon**

Pastor: During this season of Lent, we acknowledge that none of us is perfect, and none of us can do the life of faith on our own. We need God’s help, and we need each other. Therefore, we confess our sins, not as a way of beating ourselves up for our mistakes, but as a way of naming our humanness and our need before God. First, we’ll take a moment to do this personally and silently, then I’ll lead us in the prayer of confession that will be on the screens.

*Moment of silence*

**Gracious God,**  
**On Sunday the city of Jerusalem cheered for you. They laid down coats and palms for**  
**you. They shouted “Hosanna\!” and called you king. But on Friday they abandoned**  
**you. On Friday they yelled “Crucify him\!” On Friday, you were in the wilderness**  
**alone. God, we want to be Palm Sunday Christians, but we know all too well that we**  
**also are the Good Friday crowd. We claim to be faithful, inclusive, progressive; and yet we also turn our backs, exclude, and hurt your beloved children. So today we ask for forgiveness. Forgive us for the moments when we deny you and your message of love. Forgive us for preventing the spread of the gospel. Forgive us our sins.**  
**Amen.**
