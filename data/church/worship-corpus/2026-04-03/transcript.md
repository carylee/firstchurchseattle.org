# Good Friday

## Gathering
[04:00]
**Pastor Elizabeth:**
Greetings in the name of Christ, good evening.

My name is Pastor Elizabeth, my pronouns are she and her. I'm the Interim Senior Pastor here at Seattle First, and it's my honor to welcome you to this service of worship on Good Friday, as we observe when Jesus was arrested, beaten, mocked, and killed, the day when everything fell apart. The day when God fully entered human suffering. And the day that we agree not to look away from the suffering of our Savior.

It's a gift to be in worship together tonight with friends from Queen Anne UMC and Trinity UMC, and with their pastors. Reverend Katie Ladd is here from Queen Anne and Reverend Judy Schultz, the Interim Pastor from Trinity. And a Seattle First Associate, Reverend Jackie is here up in the balcony, and Reverend Cathy is here supporting us as well. And we're just so grateful to be in worship together.

I want to say a special thank you to the choirs of Seattle First and Trinity and their directors, Todd Shiveley and Elizabeth Ellis, who have worked to put this program together, which I know is going to be really meaningful to all of us.

Tonight we invite you to hear the story in a new way, and at the end of the service, to depart in silence. We won't finish out this service, there won't be a benediction, we'll leave it open-ended. And then we'll come back and complete our worship on Sunday morning at either Queen Anne or here at Seattle First at 10:30.

And now I invite you to rise in body or spirit and join me in the greeting words that are printed in your bulletin.
