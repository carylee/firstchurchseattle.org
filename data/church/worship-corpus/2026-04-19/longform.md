about seven miles from Jerusalem, and talking with each other about all
these things that had happened. While they were talking and discussing,
Jesus himself came near and went with them, but their eyes were kept
from recognizing him. And he said to them, “What are you discussing with

morning, and when they did not find his body there they came back and
told us that they had indeed seen a vision of angels who said that he
was alive. Some of those who were with us went to the tomb and found it
just as the women had said, but they did not see him.” Then he said to
them, “Oh, how foolish you are and how slow of heart to believe all that

suffer these things and then enter into his glory?” Then beginning with
Moses and all the prophets, he interpreted to them the things about

us, because it is almost evening and the day is now nearly over.” So he

bread, blessed and broke it, and gave it to them. Then their eyes were
opened, and they recognized him, and he vanished from their sight. They
said to each other, “Were not our hearts burning within us while he was

**Climate Church Covenant Wally Shaffer**

*A reading of the climate church covenant, to remind us all of our call
and commitment to take care of the earth.*

In covenant with God and with all of creation, as a climate conscious
church, the members of Seattle First United Methodist Church pledge
ourselves to be:

- responsive to our collective calling from God.

<!-- -->

- informed by the realities of the most recent climate science.

<!-- -->

- focused on the common good, including all creatures, and on treating
  future generations as we would treat ourselves.

<!-- -->

- driven by prophetic imagination as we envision a livable future and a
  just world in which we see every living being as a reflection of God’s
  majesty.

<!-- -->

- unafraid to hasten a just transition to a sustainable way of living
  that honors the interdependence of all creation

<!-- -->

- generous in using our gifts and assets, in solidarity with those
  disproportionately impacted by the effects of climate change, to build
  a more resilient community.

- prayerfully courageous in using our collective power to animate the
  conscience of the nation and to take action in the Salish Sea region,
  our state, our country, and our global community, as we join with
  people throughout the world to restore God’s glorious gift of
  creation.
