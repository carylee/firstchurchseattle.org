### Welcome and Introduction to Las Posadas
**Rev. Jackie Celin:**
As we move into our time of our Las Posadas service, I just wanted to invite any other kids that would like to participate. Anyone is welcome to participate. They can join the rest of our kids and our young adults at the back of the sanctuary there. Let us begin.

This is an introduction about the Las Posadas service. Las Posadas is a time of celebration in Mexico and the Southwestern United States. During the celebration, pilgrims journey from house to house led by angels, symbolizing the journey of Mary and Joseph, who went from house to house seeking shelter. Finally, the last house, the inn, welcomes all of the pilgrims in for prayers, feasting, and festivities. Las Posadas is a Christian, biblical, evangelistic service out of the Hispanic culture. And typically takes place for eight nights, from December 16th through December 23rd, with a special service on Christmas Eve.

Each year, groups also gather at the wall between San Diego and Tijuana, where the border meets the ocean, to do a joint service on both sides of the wall, called Las Posadas without borders, to reflect on the hope that one day, we will tear down the divisions we have created, remembering that we are all God's beloved. This service is a reimagining of the journey of the holy family, of their time spent in Bethlehem seeking shelter. In our worship service today, we are asking for full participation from you, the congregation. The intention is to imagine we are there and a part of this story, and also to consider how this story relates to our contemporary time and place. This is an Advent service. We are preparing and anticipating the birth of Jesus as we reenact and remember the moments right before Jesus's birth. As our holy family makes their way to each door, there will be a conversation that takes place between Mary and Joseph, accompanied by shepherds and angels, and the people in the home. That's all of you. You, the congregation, will fill the role of the people who answer the door, reminding us of our responsibility to welcome all that seek asylum and refuge, no matter how far from the borders we live. We invite you to participate with your whole hearts as we enter into this moment of Advent.

### Benediction: The Immigrants' Creed by Jose Luis Casal
**Rev. Wongee Joh:**
As people who have received the good news of Christ's birth and await a new, I invite us to be in solidarity with asylum seekers, with refugees, with immigrants as we share in the Immigrants' Creed as our Benediction in one voice.

**Congregation:**
I believe in a mighty God who guided the people in exile and in Exodus, the God of Joseph in Egypt and Daniel in Babylon, the God of foreigners and immigrants. I believe in Jesus Christ, a displaced Galilean, who was born away from his people and his home, who fled his country. who fled his country with his parents when his life was in danger. When he returned to his own country, he suffered under the oppression of Pontius Pilate, the servant of a foreign power. Jesus was persecuted, beaten, tortured, and unjustly condemned to death. But on the third day, Jesus rose from the dead, not as a scorned foreigner, but to offer us citizenship in God's kingdom. I believe in the Holy Spirit, the eternal immigrant from God's kingdom among us, who speaks all languages, lives in all countries, and reunites all races. I believe that the church is the secure home for foreigners and for all believers. I believe that the communion of saints begins when we embrace all God's people in all their diversity. I believe in forgiveness which makes us all equal before God, and in reconciliation which heals our brokenness. I believe that in the resurrection, God will reunite us as one people in which all are distinct and all are alike at the same time. I believe in eternal life in which no one will forever, but all will be citizens of the kingdom where God reigns forever and ever. Amen.
